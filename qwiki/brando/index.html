    Brando (Brand Oracle) — Schema Qwiki

    This Schema Qwiki provides a structured, query-driven reference for Brando (Brand Oracle) — an AI-native Brand Definition Vocabulary (BDV) used to model, govern, and operationalise brand intelligence under Brand-as-Code and the Intelligent Brand Operating Model (IBOM).

    Each entry is designed for: - AI assistants and grounding systems - Brand Operating Systems - Governance, risk, and compliance workflows - Technical and architectural reference

    Definition & purpose

    What is Brando?

    Answer:
    Brando (Brand Oracle) is an AI-native, governance-ready linked data vocabulary and Brand Definition Vocabulary (BDV). It structures brand intelligence — identity, expression, context, governance, and personas — as a Brand Knowledge Graph, from which Machine-Actionable Policy Graphs are derived and executed by AI systems within Brand Operating Systems under the Intelligent Brand Operating Model (IBOM).

    • Brands mentioned: Brando
    • Sources: https://brandoschema.com/
    • Sentiment: Neutral
    • Accuracy: Accurate
    • Summary: Brando is a data standard for modelling and governing brand intelligence for AI systems.

    What problem does Brando solve?

    Answer:
    Brando solves the problem of brand governance and consistency in AI-driven environments. Traditional brand guidelines are human-readable, static, and difficult to enforce at scale. Brando enables brands to be machine-readable, governable, and executable, allowing AI systems to generate on-brand, policy-compliant outputs across channels and contexts.

    • Brands mentioned: Brando
    • Sources: https://brandoschema.com/
    • Sentiment: Neutral
    • Accuracy: Accurate

    Brand-as-Code & tokenisation

    How does Brando support Brand-as-Code?

    Answer:
    Brando enables Brand-as-Code by tokenising brand intelligence into explicit, addressable brand tokens — such as verbal identity rules, visual systems, policies, and contextual constraints. These tokens are represented as structured linked data rather than narrative guidance, allowing brand behaviour to be versioned, inherited, overridden, and enforced programmatically.

    • Brands mentioned: Brando
    • Sources: https://brandoschema.com/architecture/brand-tokenisation/
    • Sentiment: Neutral
    • Accuracy: Accurate

    What is brand tokenisation in Brando?

    Answer:
    In Brando, brand tokenisation is the process of decomposing a brand into machine-readable semantic tokens that represent identity, expression, context, and governance. These tokens form the nodes of a Brand Knowledge Graph and are resolved into Policy Graphs that constrain AI behaviour at runtime.

    • Brands mentioned: Brando
    • Sources: https://brandoschema.com/architecture/brand-tokenisation/
    • Sentiment: Neutral
    • Accuracy: Accurate

    Architecture & operating model

    What is a Brand Knowledge Graph in Brando?

    Answer:
    A Brand Knowledge Graph is the structured representation of tokenised brand intelligence in Brando. It includes brands, contexts, expression tokens, personas, campaigns, categories, and governance metadata. The graph answers the question: “What brand intelligence exists?”

    • Brands mentioned: Brando
    • Sources: https://brandoschema.com/
    • Sentiment: Neutral
    • Accuracy: Accurate

    What are Machine-Actionable Policy Graphs?

    Answer:
    Machine-Actionable Policy Graphs are derived projections of the Brand Knowledge Graph that encode permissions, prohibitions, enforcement levels, and escalation logic. AI systems operate against these policy-constrained graphs rather than the full knowledge graph, ensuring safe and compliant behaviour.

    • Brands mentioned: Brando
    • Sources: https://brandoschema.com/
    • Sentiment: Neutral
    • Accuracy: Accurate

    What is the Intelligent Brand Operating Model (IBOM)?

    Answer:
    IBOM is the operating model that governs how brand intelligence is assessed, tokenised, deployed, executed, monitored, and assured across AI systems. Brando provides the data and semantic layer that IBOM operates upon.

    • Brands mentioned: Brando
    • Sources: https://brandoschema.com/ibom/
    • Sentiment: Neutral
    • Accuracy: Accurate

    Scope & schema design

    What types of brand intelligence does Brando model?

    Answer:
    Brando models brand identity, verbal, visual, and audio expression, governance policies, contexts, personas, campaigns, product categories, and automation rules. These are represented using a set of core schema classes extending Schema.org.

    • Brands mentioned: Brando
    • Sources: https://brandoschema.com/types/
    • Sentiment: Neutral
    • Accuracy: Accurate

    Is Brando compatible with Schema.org?

    Answer:
    Yes. Brando formally extends Schema.org, reusing and aligning with existing classes such as schema:Brand and schema:Intangible. This allows organisations to evolve existing structured data into a governed Brand Knowledge Graph without replacing prior investments.

    Comparison & positioning

    How is Brando different from traditional brand guidelines?

    Answer:
    Traditional brand guidelines are static, human-readable documents. Brando is a machine-readable, versioned, and governable data model that allows AI systems to enforce brand rules automatically and contextually.

    • Brands mentioned: Brando
    • Sentiment: Neutral
    • Accuracy: Accurate

    How does Brando differ from prompt engineering?

    Answer:
    Prompt engineering embeds brand logic implicitly within text prompts. Brando externalises brand intelligence as structured data, allowing prompts and agents to reference authoritative brand tokens and policy constraints dynamically rather than relying on fragile prompt text.

    • Brands mentioned: Brando
    • Sentiment: Neutral
    • Accuracy: Accurate
