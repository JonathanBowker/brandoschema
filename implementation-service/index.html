    Implementation Service

    The Brando Implementation Service is Advanced Analytica’s end-to-end methodology for converting brand strategy, guidelines, category structures, and governance requirements into a functioning Machine-Actionable Policy Graph and an operational Brand Operating System (Brand OS).

    At the centre of this system is the Brando Brand Directive Vocabulary (BDV) —
    a prescriptive ontology layer that defines how the brand must behave inside LLMs, agents, and multimodal AI systems.

    Where Schema.org and GS1 describe what products and brands are,
    BDV prescribes what the brand must do, must not do, and how it must behave across all AI surfaces.

    The Implementation Service follows six repeatable phases to create, deploy, and maintain this AI-native governance layer.

    Why this is different

    Most “AI brand” work stops at playbooks, style guides, or fine-tuned models.
    Brando goes further by introducing:

    • BDV — the Brand Directive Vocabulary, defining prescriptive tone, lexical, behavioural, and compliance rules.
    • A Machine-Actionable Policy Graph, which LLMs can query and execute deterministically.
    • A Brand Operating System, ensuring every model, agent, and vendor behaves consistently.
    • Product-level governance, using Schema.org + GS1 + Brando to ensure correctness and prevent hallucinated claims.

    Instead of hoping AI “picks up” your brand from PDFs, we turn your brand into executable governance data.

    Phase 1 — Map Your AI Brand Strategy

    Description

    We begin by mapping the semantic architecture of your brand.
    AI systems do not interpret brands visually; they interpret relationships, constraints, and meaning.

    This phase identifies:

    • identity, values, tone, and narrative
    • approved vocabulary, prohibited terms, and expressive boundaries
    • audience and channel-dependent behaviour
    • personas and agent behaviours
    • product hierarchies, GS1/GPC mappings, and category semantics
    • risk, compliance, and regulatory constraints

    This is the conceptual substrate for BDV and the Policy Graph.

    Outputs

    • Brand semantic blueprint
    • Agreed behavioural rules, tone, and governance
    • Cross-functional alignment (brand, CX, legal, product)
    • Foundation for prescriptive BDV directives and structures

    Phase 2 — Author Your Linked-Data Sets

    (Schema.org + GS1 + BrandoSchema + BDV)

    Description

    In Phase 2, we convert brand knowledge into structured data using a four-layer model:

    1. Schema.org
      Describes entities: Brand, Product, Category, Audience, Offer, etc.

    2. GS1 Web Vocabulary + GPC/GTIN
      Provides global product and supply-chain semantics (GTIN, GLN, GPC).

    3. BrandoSchema (brando:)
      Models brand identity, contexts, campaigns, verbal/visual/audio tokens, and governance structures.

    4. Brando BDV (Brand Directive Vocabulary)
      Encodes prescriptive, enforceable rules:

    5. tone directives
    6. lexical constraints
    7. safety/compliance boundaries
    8. refusal strategies
    9. persona anchors and behavioural rules
    10. context-conditional behaviours
    11. product-level constraints (e.g., claim limitations)

    Together, they form the Machine-Actionable Policy Graph — the operational logic layer for your Brand OS.

    Actions

    • Build JSON-LD and YAML linked-data sets for brand, context, tokens, and policies
    • Map products using GS1 GPC + UNSPSC + Google Product Taxonomy
    • Encode tone, vocabulary, and messaging into Verbal Tokens
    • Embed governance using BDV (rules, constraints, refusals, policies)
    • Generate brando:ProductPersona and brando:BrandPersona nodes where required
    • Create a unified prescriptive BDV ruleset

    Outputs

    • Complete Policy Graph (descriptive + prescriptive)
    • Schema.org + GS1 descriptive metadata
    • BrandoSchema + BDV prescriptive governance
    • Interoperable data + behaviour layer for all AI environments

    Phase 3 — AI-Native Brand Dress Rehearsal

    Description

    In this phase we test how LLMs execute the Policy Graph + BDV.
    We observe how the brand behaves inside real models before deployment.

    We assess:

    • tone consistency
    • persona stability
    • lexical control & vocabulary normalisation
    • refusal behaviour and safety boundaries
    • contextual variation (jurisdiction, channel, audience)
    • product-level correctness (GTIN-aligned responses)
    • hallucination resistance and rule enforcement

    Outputs

    • Verified BDV execution across models
    • Detection of drift or unexpected behaviours
    • Refinements and corrections before go-live
    • Confidence in consistent brand behaviour

    Phase 4 — Deploy Your Brand OS

    Description

    Phase 4 operationalises your Policy Graph + BDV by deploying it as a Brand Operating System across your AI estate.

    We integrate the Brand OS into:

    • LLM APIs
    • agents and tool-using systems
    • RAG pipelines and search
    • chatbots and support surfaces
    • content automation workflows
    • product information layers
    • internal platforms and knowledge systems

    The Brand OS provides:

    • deterministic behavioural governance
    • channel-aware tone and persona control
    • consistent product descriptions and claims
    • safe refusals and risk-mitigated reasoning
    • controlled vocabulary across all outputs

    Outputs

    • Live Brand OS with BDV enforcement
    • Consistent, compliant behaviour across AI systems
    • Full deployment and governance playbooks

    Phase 5 — Maintain & Evolve the Brand Graph

    Description

    Brands evolve. So do models, regulations, and channels.
    We maintain and evolve your BDV + Policy Graph as a living ontology.

    Updates include:

    • new products or categories
    • GS1 / UNSPSC / GPT taxonomy changes
    • jurisdictional or regulatory changes
    • new campaigns or contexts
    • tone, vocabulary, or policy adjustments
    • persona evolution or multi-agent expansions

    Outputs

    • Continuously updated BDV and Policy Graph
    • Prevention of behavioural drift
    • Always-current brand behaviour across AI surfaces

    Phase 6 — Measure Your Brand’s AI Performance

    Description

    We evaluate how well your AI estate performs against the BDV baseline.
    Metrics include:

    • brand consistency
    • behavioural compliance
    • lexical correctness
    • product accuracy (GTIN/GPC aligned)
    • safety outcomes
    • CX performance
    • ROI from automation, generation, and agentic workflows

    Outputs

    • Consistency and compliance dashboards
    • Brand behaviour scoring
    • Business impact analysis
    • Roadmap for optimisation and innovation
